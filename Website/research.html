Z is for Zoning: Market Tightness and the Price Effects of Land Use Regulation

Undergraduate Thesis · University of Virginia · April 2026

This paper examines the relationship between changes in land use regulations and housing prices across U.S. municipalities. To measure the change in regulatory intensity, I utilize the Wharton Residential Land Use Regulatory Index (WRLURI) from 2006 and 2018. I regress municipal-level housing price growth, measured as the real percentage change in the FHFA House Price Index matched to the municipal level, on the change in regulatory intensity. I examine how the relationship between regulatory changes and price growth varies with initial vacancy rates, testing whether market tightness moderates the observed association with regulatory restrictions. The results indicate that municipalities experiencing increased regulatory restrictiveness are associated with higher real housing price growth, with this relationship notably stronger in areas initially characterized by low vacancy rates.
